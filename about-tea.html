About Tea



Black Tea

Black tea is the most common tea and exist in many different variety


Green Tea

Green tea is another very common tea and is often used as a healthy alternative to black tea, even though their isn't any evidence to support it


Rooibos Tea

Red tea or Roobios that it is also called is a tea alternative that has became very common last 10-15 years in the west as a non caffeine alternative and isn't technically a real tea


White Tea

White tea is a tea that has started to become more common in cafes last 5 years in the west and is considered an even healtier alternative to green tea


Yellow Tea

Yellow tea is very uncommon and you won't find it outside special tea shops online and very special shops in some cities


Oolong Tea

Oolong tea which means Dark Dragon Tea is between green and black tea and is often a hue of grey or brown colour


Blue Tea

Blue tea which is also named Butterfly Pea Tea is a very uncommon tea that you only find in very special shops online and is very uncommon in even very special shops in cities, if you change the pH value in the tea with example a lemon it will turn from blue to purple


Purple Tea

Purple tea is not the same tea as the Blue tea and even comes from Kenya as a special mutation, this tea turns pink when you add lemon and of is just as uncommon as the blue tea
